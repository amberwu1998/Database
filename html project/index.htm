Biological Databases

Our platform is a centralized hub for biological databases and provides invaluable access to data resources, fostering discovery and innovation. We make your navigation and discovery of key resources easy!
Database Name Database Example Brief Description Icon
DNA Database Genbank A database of DNA profiles which can be used in the analysis of genetic diseases, genetic fingerprinting for criminology, or genetic genealogy.
Gene Expression Database GEO DataSets A database record the levels of gene activity across different conditions, tissues, or developmental stages, aiding researchers in understanding how genes are regulated and how they contribute to biological processes.
Genome Database Genome NIH A database contain complete genome sequences of various organisms, facilitating research on genome structure, function, and evolution. They serve as invaluable resources for studying genetic variations, identifying genes associated with diseases, and understanding evolutionary relationships.
Phenotype Database dbGap A database document observable traits resulting from genetic interactions, providing insights into how genetic variations manifest as physical or biochemical characteristics in organisms. They are essential for studying genotype-phenotype relationships and identifying genetic factors underlying complex traits and diseases.
Radiologic Database ACR A database store medical images, such as X-rays, CT scans, and MRIs, along with associated clinical data. These databases support healthcare professionals in diagnosing diseases, planning treatments, and monitoring patient progress through the visualization and analysis of medical images.
